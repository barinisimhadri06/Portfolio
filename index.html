About Me

Ever since I was young, I’ve been fascinated by the stories hidden in data those patterns and insights that can change everything. This curiosity led me to earn a Bachelor’s in Business Administration and continue with a Master’s in Business Analytics at DePaul University. I love learning new things, and my education was filled with that passion for diving deep into the world of numbers. Over 5 years as a Data Analyst, I was like a detective for data. I cleaned messy datasets, used tools like Python and R to build models, and created dashboards in Tableau and Power BI that turned complex information into clear insights for our clients. I also worked with big data tools like Hadoop and Spark to tackle large datasets. My work helped clients in India, the USA, and the UK improve their digital experiences and make smarter decisions. I’m always eager to learn and grow. I’ve earned certifications in Data Science from Google and Microsoft. I enjoy keeping up with the latest tools and techniques, making sure I’m always at the cutting edge of my field. At the heart of everything I do is a genuine love for data.

Fradualent Transaction Detection

Utilizing machine learning techniques like Isolation Forest and Local Outlier Factor, this project aims to detect fraudulent transactions in the banking industry in real-time. Isolation Forest, an unsupervised algorithm, identifies anomalies based on the 'few and different' principle using randomly sub-sampled data processed in tree structures. Meanwhile, LOF, another unsupervised algorithm, identifies outliers by evaluating local density, focusing on points deviating from local clusters rather than the entire data distribution in order to enhance fraud detection measures.

Demand Prediction of Drivers Availability

This project addresses the task of forecasting driver hours through a multi-step time series approach. It employs several strategies including direct, recursive, hybrid, and multiple-output forecasting. These methods aim to predict future values in a time series by leveraging predictive models based on current values. The process involves dataset preparation, feature breakdown for time series analysis, and the application of predictive models to predict subsequent time steps in a step-by-step manner.
